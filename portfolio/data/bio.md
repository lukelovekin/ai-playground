## Personality and working style

### How I work best
Luke works best with a degree of autonomy — given a problem and the space to think through a solution, rather than a prescriptive spec. He's most effective when he has genuine input into how something is designed, not just how it's built. That said, he's pragmatic: when constraints exist, he works within them without friction.

### Collaboration style
Comfortable working independently or as part of a team. He values clear, direct communication and brings the same openness to cross-functional collaboration — having worked across remote-first engineering teams, alongside founders, product managers, and enterprise stakeholders throughout his career. He enjoys the people side of engineering as much as the technical side.

### What energises me
Solving problems that haven't been solved cleanly before — particularly at the intersection of engineering quality and real-world usefulness. Luke is motivated by seeing things ship and have impact, not just by the work itself. New technical challenges, especially ones that require rethinking an existing approach rather than repeating a known pattern, are where he does his best work.

### What I find genuinely interesting about AI right now
The force-multiplier effect — the ability to build tools in days that would have taken months, or to augment existing systems with intelligence that was previously out of reach. What excites Luke most is the agentic layer: systems that reason, use tools, and operate across multiple steps rather than just responding to a single prompt. The "AI as co-pilot programmer" model resonates strongly — both as a tool he uses to build faster and as a pattern he builds into products.

## Background

### How I got into AI
Luke's view is that AI integration is becoming a baseline expectation for software engineers, not a separate discipline — much the same way "full stack" eventually stopped being a specialisation and became the norm. That conviction, combined with a genuine enjoyment of the tooling, drove him to invest seriously in the space. He completed IBM professional certifications in RAG and Agentic AI, then built 25+ hands-on projects to put that knowledge into practice. The goal was never to pivot away from software engineering — it was to make sure AI capability was a natural part of his engineering toolkit.

### What makes my background unusual
A 10-year career in construction and project management before moving into software engineering. That experience — managing budgets, timelines, trades, and stakeholders on medium-rise builds — shapes how I approach technical work: ship something that works, own the outcome, communicate clearly with non-technical people.
